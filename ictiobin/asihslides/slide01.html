The genus Carpiodes has long been a taxonomically troublesome group. Milton Trautman said that few genera show such diverse morphological types as do carpsuckers. The high degree of sympatry and morphological variability, and the lack of adequate study material has contributed to the taxonomic confusion within the group. At present only three taxa are currently recognized (C. velifer, C. carpio, and C. cyprinus), however the ranges of all three are believed to contain undescribed or unrecognized forms.
