GROUP PROJECTS

Your grade in this course will be based in part on a project researched by small teams of students and presented to the class toward the end of the semester. You will work in assigned groups. Confer with members of your group to decide on one of the topics below. You will need instructor approval for alternative topics. Research the topic in the library and using the Internet and decide as a group how you will subdivide the workload. An outline of your project is due October 31st. The outline should detail the subtopics to be covered and list the information sources.

POSSIBLE TOPICS

Instream Flow Incremental Methodology (IFIM)

Index of Biotic Integrity (IBI)

Stream invertebrates as indicators of environmental stress

Effects of rainfall patterns and watershed characteristics on surface runoff

Regulated rivers (effects of reservoir storage and hydropower peaking on stream flow patterns)

Thermal pollution in streams (cold water release, use of streams for cooling water)

Effects of impoundments on sediment transport dynamics in streams

Navigation impacts on streams (locks, dams, dredging)

Agricultural land use impacts on stream ecosystems

Impacts of urbanization/urban sprawl on headwater streams

Long term flow patterns in the southeastern U.S.

Case studies of problems of large rivers (Columbia River, Colorado River, Kissimmee River)

Demonstration project on use of BASINS software
