Adaptations: Aquatic Insects
•  No single line of aquatic insect evolution.
•  Insects invaded lotic environments multiple times and in different
ways.
•  Thus, insects have solved the problem of living in freshwaters
multiple times.
•  Since streams contain much less oxygen (1/200th) than terrestrial
systems, many adaptations have evolved for living underwater.
•  Land insects use tracheal system to respire.  Does not work
underwater…..
