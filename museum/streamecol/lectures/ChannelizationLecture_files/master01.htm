Topic for the next couple of days :
Stream modification – any physical modification of a stream by humans
3 forms of modification:
Impoundments
Water Removal/Diversion
Channelization
Channelizing --- make stream uniform by the techniques
Question for class:  Why do we channelize?
Decreases lateral extent of a stream (think flood plain, full bank width, etc.), increase flow:  Flood Control
Irrigation for aggricultural lands
Drainage of farm land, urban areas
navigation
Straightens the mainstem
reduce meandering
increase slope of stream
As a result of channilization:
higher flow velocities
higher erosional energy
downcutting of the channel (causes stream to become entrenched i.e. more confined to mainstem, therefore preventing further meandering)
stream length is reduced (What does this mean? increase in stream gradient & flow velocity: impacts OPM processing, nutrient spiraling, less habitat for biota)
Some examples of channilization
Energy doesn’t dissipate as well as in un-altered streams
increase erosion & bank scouring
results in increased suspended settlement & beadload carried downstream increased sedimentation downstream (Aggradation) aggradation disturbes habitats and increases flood potential increased sediment, increases turbidity: What are some consequences of this? impacts primary production impacts water quality impacts other types of food/nutrient supplies to stream organisms
examples ctd.
Because velocities increase:
affect sediment particle size distribution along stream bed
loss of organic materials (physical removal or velocity)
substrate is more uniform (lack pools, riffles, & other diverse characteristics of unaltered streams) because of the loss of organic materials and substrates that may encourage deposition of sediment, also reduces habitat diversity
examples, final
Lowers water table in surrounding floodplain
Reduces exchange between hyporheic and surface flow
velocity increases causing downcutting of the channel, lowering of the mainstem water table therefore, less water stored in the floodplain, depriving lateral habitats of cool groundwater as groundwater is drained, soil gets compacted, reducing the soil’s ability to store water hyporheic recharge from the stream is also reduced as the groundwater stores reduce
Impacts on macroinverts and fishes:
reduces instream vegetation
disrupts riffle-pool sequence
reduces overall stream length
increase gradient flow and velocity
dewaters adjacent lands
 Combination of these leads to:
Instream substrate unstable
benthic inverts cannot become established
reduces spawning substrate for fishes
Increased sediment particles
abrasive effect on fish & macroinvert gills
Interstitial spaces become clogged
removing retreat for inverts
etc
Different ways to channelize:
Dredging: makes the substrate/sides uniform, changes natural structure of pool-riffle mannually
Different ways to channelize ctd.:
removal of tree stumps and large woody debris
significant b/c: large tree falls very important in regulating stream habitat diversity obstruct stream flow: allow coarse substrate to build up, downstream scouring which results in pools Woody debris is shelter/cover for fishes, spawning substrate for some (Cyprinella venusta), food source for inverts
Different ways to channelize ctd.:
Total removal of riparian vegitation, to build up walls that shore up the dredged channel
Riparian veg is an important source of woody debris
removal of canopy reduces shading, therefore increases stream temps.
leaf supply disrupted
increase in light would do what? increase primary prod. innitially
when tree roots are removed, banks more suseptable to erosion
reduces ability of floodplain to attenuate flood waters
Some human-related misfortunes due to channilization:
Increase in Erosion (homes, roads, bridges, etc...)
Some human-related misfortunes due to channilization:
Increase in flooding
