Influences on Stream Biota
34
What happens to Algae if there are too many nutrients?
  • When a stream contains more then natural amount of Nutrients from runoff Eutrophication can occur!
    • Eutrophication is a condition in an aquatic ecosystem where high nutrient concentrations stimulate blooms of algae (e.g., phytoplankton).
    • This may sound good for the algae, but its bad for everyone else.
35
What about if there is too much Sediment?
  • Too much sediment from erosion or runoff can cause turbidity!


  • This means that Algae can’t get any sun and can’t grow!
36
What Does Pollution do to Algae?
  • The obvious answer would be to kill it! And that’s exactly what it does!!


  • Also Chlorine pollution has been seen to lower algal diversity in some streams
37
So what does all that stuff do to Macro invertebrates?
  • Nutrient loading can cause biomass to increase, but this can mean the DO can decrease. That means that inverts that need normal amounts of DO will be replaced with inverts that don’t!
  • Nutrient loading can also result in an increase in size and richness of inverts that feed on the Algae, that has increased as a result of the nutrients.
38
What about Sediment?
  • Sediment can effect invert’s in a few ways!
    • Sediment can change substrate, therefore altering their habitat.
    • Sediment can block out sun affecting the algal community and therein affecting the invert’s that eat algae.
    • Physical abrasion of body surfaces
      • Increases susceptibility to infection
    • Filter feeding of macroinvertebrates
  • These changes can not only effect populations but can also effect the invertebrate diversity!
39
And Pollution?
  • Pollution hurts invertebrates by affecting what they eat and where they live.
  • Inverts get pollution through eating autotrophs that have absorbed it.
  • Also pollution can lower water quality such as adding acidity or lowering DO, these too can kill inverts.
40
How about the Fish?
  • Because Streams are usually nutrient limiting an increase in nutrients like N and P can be beneficial to all fauna.
  • Yet if nutrients get too high then the stream will be choked by algae which will lower the DO and could change diversity, having a bottom up trophic affect.
  • Result no food or bad food = DEAD Fish!
41
What Can Dirty Water Do?
  • Sediment can alter the stream substrate and change the habitats available.
  • Also sediment can alter food types as mentioned before.
  • In one case increased sediment caused a decrease in species that did not clean their nests, and an increase in sediment “tolerant” fish.
  • Like Sunfish!!
42
What About pollution?
  • Pollution could cause a 3 eyed fish but I haven’t seen any evidence yet!
  • Mostly pollution cause changes in water pH and nutrient levels. This of course alters the type of fish that are present.
  • Also, pollution can cause not only declines in the fish themselves, but also on the populations of their food. Often causing the decline in the fish.
