IBI

  • Advantages

  • takes into account different aspects of the biotic community

  • chemical monitoring systems miss many anthropogenic disturbances, such as: flow alteration, habitat degradation, etc.

  • impossible to take into account all factors that influence biotic integrity

  • takes into account changes over time

  • measures short-term events that may effect biotic integrity, such as flooding or short lived events that may be seasonal or manmade

  • Disadvantages

  • limited regional use of IBI

  • proportions of different fish can be affected by seasonality, food supply, etc.

  • sampling techniques vary, samples obtained dependent upon techniques utilized

  • areas of streams may be more conducive to certain sampling techniques than other areas
