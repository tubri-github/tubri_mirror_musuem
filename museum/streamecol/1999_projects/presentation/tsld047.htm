    Elassomatidae (Pygmy Sunfishes): Greater in ‘post’
  • ?? Occur in areas with little or no current with silt/mud substrates.
  • Deposit eggs in aquatic vegetation, not directly on the bottom in ‘cleared nests’
    Fundulidae (Topminnows/Killifish): More in ‘post’
  • Found in wide variety of habitats (pool/riffle)
  • Live near the surface of the water (less vulnerable to scouring flows on the stream bed?)
  • “tough” fish
  • Of the 4 total species collected, the blackstripe topminnow (Fundulus notatus) and blackspotted topminnow (Fundulus olivaceus) were the most abundant. F. olivaceus prefers slower flows (found more in ‘pre’ collections, while F. notatus was slightly more abundant in the ‘post’ collections.
