    Impacts on Stream Macroinvertebrates

    Richness of invertebrate communities is influenced by several factors altered by reservoir construction, such as flow, substrate, and aquatic vegetation.
    Ward (1976) identified four types of flow regime:
    • Reduced flow: low current speed allow fine particles to settle into interstitial areas within the substratum; this can reduce habitat availability for some species.
    • Seasonal flow constancy: bed and bank stability, clarity due to sediment deposition, and more constant currents are generally favorable for vegetation, and consequently, to many benthic invertebrates.
    • Increased flow: results in scouring of the stream bottom, leading to losses in vegetation and FPOM utilized as food resources. This will reduce or eliminate some benthic species.
    • Short-term flow fluctuations: cause dramatic effects such as stranding of biota during low flows or downstream displacement of benthos. Variable current speeds may destroy pool/riffle sequences and interfere with feeding techniques.
