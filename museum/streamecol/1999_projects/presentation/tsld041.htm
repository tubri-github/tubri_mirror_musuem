    Impacts on Terrestrial Organisms

    Streamside vegetation is an essential resource for many land-dwelling animals, and its destruction can lead to many adverse effects.
  • Many organisms utilize streamside habitats seasonally for shelter, food, and reproductive resources. Elks give birth in riparian areas, while reptiles and amphibians often lay their eggs near the stream’s edge.
  • Birds often use the shores for nesting, egg-laying, and roosting. Reservoirs may lead to destruction of trees, and fluctuating water levels can wash away eggs or destroy nests.
  • There is often a correlation between the destruction of these habitats and the decline in animal populations, such as minks, muskrats, beavers, river otters, and nutria.
