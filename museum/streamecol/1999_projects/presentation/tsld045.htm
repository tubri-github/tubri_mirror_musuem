    Lepisosteidae (Gars): More abundant in pre-impoundment.
  • Eggs fall to the bottom of the stream, where they adhere to the substrate or aquatic vegetation..
  • Larvae are not strong swimmers (may have trouble with high flows)
  • Spotted gar (Lepisosteus oculatus) prefer low flows or no current and were collected most frequently in the pre-dam period. Longnose gar (Lepisosteus osseus) frequently inhabit rivers and reservoirs and seem to prefer flowing water. They were more abundant in the post-impoundment samples.
    Sciaenidae (Drums): Appeared in ‘post’ - absent in ‘pre-’
  • Important estuarine/brackish fish; freshwater drum (Aplodinotus grunniens)
  • Smaller individuals are common in marginal areas of rivers, reservoirs, and their tributaries. Larger sciaenids prefer the fast currents below dams.
  • Pelagic spawners, floating eggs
  • Restricted distribution of freshwater mussels- host: A. grunniens
