    Percidae (Perches and Darters): Greater in pre-impoundment
  • Very speciose (second richest family behind Cyprinidae)
  • Occupy a variety of habitats, but some species are very specialized, making it more difficult to find and adjust to varied conditions.
  • “Dart” along on the bottom of streams and lakes; sometimes known as ‘crawly bottoms’ because they use their pelvic and caudal fins to rest and move around in the substrate.
  • Consume benthic organisms.
  • Various spawning strategies: 1) egg buriers; 2) egg clumpers; 3) attachers --oriented towards substrate.
  • Effects from increased flows- disturbs benthos
  • Percina aurora
