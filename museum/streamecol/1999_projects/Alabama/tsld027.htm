The Alabama River

  • formed by the union of the Tallapoosa and Coosa Rivers, 10 miles northeast of Montgomery, Alabama

  • Navigable through out 315 mile waterway

  • Drains 22,600 square miles

  • 5 1/2 inch per mile fall above Montgomery, 4 inch per mile fall below Montgomery

  • Joins the Tombigbee River 44 miles north of Mobile

  • Contains 3 navigational lock and dam structures- Claiborne, Millers Ferry, and the Robert F. Henry Lock and Dams

  • 3 lakes are formed by these structures- William Dannelly Lake, R.E. Woodruff Lake, and Claiborne Lake

  • The Alabama River is the 16th largest river in the United States
