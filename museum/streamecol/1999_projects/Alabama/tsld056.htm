Effects on Biota

  • Organisms adapted to river cycles of peaks and lows will suffer due to habitat changes which occur due to stream regulation or stunted flow

  • Certain species niches will no longer exist in areas, such as eddies or rapids

  • Introduction of new species to reservoirs create new competition- ex. Lakes stocked for recreational fishing

  • Increased sediment loads or fall outs along with reduction of riparian vegetation will decrease numbers of shredders and scavengers

  • Prevents upstream spawning and the movement of biota to upstream habitats
