The Pascagoula River Data

  • The discharge data for the Pascagoula River came from a gauging station in Merrill, MS.

  • This location was chosen because it is does not have any impoundments altering its flow, it lies between the Red and Alabama Rivers, and has accessible data.

  • There are no locks or impoundments of any kind on this river.

  • The average discharge of the Pascagoula River at Merrill, MS, is ~9,600 cfs.

  • There was only one five year subset, because there was no impoundments built on this river

  • The discharge data appeared to follow a pattern of higher discharge from December through May and lower discharge between June and November, similar to the discharge of the Alabama River.
