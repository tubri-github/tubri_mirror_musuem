Red River History

  • Log raft removed 1873 for navigation choked 160 miles of river in Louisiana

  • Denison Dam and Lake Texoma created at mile 726 in 1943 for flood control and hydropower

  • 1946 Flood Control Act- called for 6 flood control reservoirs in order to lower flow line and reduce the variability of flow because of the reservoir lag

  • Red River waterway authorized, 1968- 236 mile long navigation channel maintained 9ft deep, 200ft wide with 84foot wide lock and dam structures

  • Channel straightening, levee bank stabilization and dredging occour along the river resulting in an incision of the embankment such that a 50 year flood will be contained
