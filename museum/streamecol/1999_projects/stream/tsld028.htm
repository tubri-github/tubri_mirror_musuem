Biotic Response to Floods

  • Algae: Rhizoclonium persisted after floods due to basal attachments to substrate, however, over time recolonization was dominated by more filamentous Spirogyra (Power and Stewart 1986).

  • Invertebrates will seek refugia during floods, for protection from downstream export and scouring, in stream margins and rock eddies. Species adapted to floods may have increased developmental rates and subsequent reduced body size (Poff and Ward 1989)
