Conclusions

  • Stream flow is increasing in the southeastern U.S.

  • The increase appears to be fairly constant.

  • Seasonally, the months of January through June show substantially increased stream flow.

  • Relative to the 1940’s, no trend was found in recent occurrence of extreme weather events.

  • However, standard deviations of the mean monthly flows for January, February, and April show more variability in recent decades.

  • More study and better statistical analyses are highly recommended.
