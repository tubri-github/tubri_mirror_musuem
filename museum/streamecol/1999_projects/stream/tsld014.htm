Case Studies

    Vegetation: attached algae is most abundant in fast water with hard substrates, rooted vascular plants in slow water with soft substrates. (Allan 1995)
    Caddisfly larvae: species are adapted to construct nets at specific current velocities. (Edington 1968)
    Blacktail shiner: preferred spawning current speed varies across streams. Too low a flow risks eggs being covered by sediments and suffering reduced oxygen availability. Too high may prevent complete insemination, egg positioning, and adherence to substrate. (Baker, Killgore, and Foster 1994)
