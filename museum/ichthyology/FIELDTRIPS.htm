Field Trips

 

We will make four field trips over the course of the semester.  Two of the trips will be to local streams, generally within one-hour's drive of New Orleans.  Check the lab schedule for dates. On these days, you should come prepared to wade in waste-deep water and mud. I suggest that you wear cutoff shorts and/or a swimsuit, and shoes you don't mind getting wet and muddy.  Bring along a backpack with a change of clothes, or at least a towel and dry shoes.  We will probably sample more than one stream and will likely return to campus late on these days.  

 

We will also make a weekend trip to St. Andrews State Park in Panama City Beach, Florida.  We will leave campus at 1 p.m. on the day the trip is scheduled.  We will return to campus late following Sunday.  The focus of this trip will be marine fishes and primitive large river fishes.

 

Lastly, we will make a trip to the Aquarium of the Americas on the last lab day.  This trip will give you the chance to apply your new knowledge of fish diversity and biology, while viewing living specimens of a wide variety of fishes.
