Percina copelandi, (Jordan, 1877)

Channel darter

 

© Noel Burkhead & Virginia Dept of Game and Inland Fisheries (Fishes of Virginia

 

Synonymy:

Rheocrypta copelandi, Jordan, 1877

 

Conservation Status:

Louisiana species of conservation concern. 

Agencies are focusing on developing effective monitoring techniques.

 

Federal T&E Status: Not evaluated

State Ranking: S1S2

NatureServe Global Ranking: G4

 

 

Habitat and Biology

Distribution:  Eastern North American watersheds; ranges from Canada to St. Lawrence-Great Lakes region south to Louisiana.  Mississippi, Pearl, Pascagoula and Mobile drainages.

 

Habitat: Freshwater; inhabits sandy/gravel bottoms in pools and riffles of small to medium streams and rivers, as well as, lakes.  Usually found along shorelines and in relatively clear water.

 

Biology:

Feeding: Feeds on aquatic insects.

 

Reproduction: Spawns April-May in Kansas and probably Missouri and Virginia, June-July in Michigan, April-June in Oklahoma (Hubbs 1985, Page 1983, Burkhead and Jenkins 1991; see also Suttkus et al. 1994), at water temperatures of 20-21 C (Burkhead and Jenkins 1991). Females mature at age one. Spawning is communal, with many territories of breeding males in a small area. Spawning generally occurs over gravel, rubble, or rock-strewn bedrock in moderate or swift current. Occurs and spawns also along lake shores in the northeastern part of the range.

 

Morphology:

Body Shape: Snout short and blunt, mouth small.

Maximum size: 7.2 cm TL (male/unsexed) 

 

Life Colors:  Greenish gray with several small dark spots connected by a thin line along sides. X-marks on scales on upper parts of sides. No orange band in dorsal fin.
