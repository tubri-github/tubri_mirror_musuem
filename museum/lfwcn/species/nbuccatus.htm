Louisiana Fish Species of Concern Status Report

 

 

Notropis buccatus, (Cope, 1865)

Silverjaw minnow

 

picture (Nobuc_u0.jpg) by The Native Fish Conservancy

 

Synonymy:

Ericymba buccata, Cope, 1865

 

Conservation Status:

Are considered critically imperiled in two states New York and Tennessee. 

Impoundments are thought to be the main factor fragmenting populations.

 

Federal T&E Status: Not evaluated

State Ranking: S2S4

NatureServe Global Ranking: G5

 

Habitat and Biology

Distribution:  Northeastern United States around the great lakes. Found to occur in the Mississippi River, Ohio River, and most Atlantic coast tributaries north of the Cumberland River.

 

Map of distribution within Louisiana:

 

Habitat: Freshwater; occur in shallow sandy bottoms in swift flowing riffles of clear streams and medium rivers.                                           

 

Biology: Benthic; adults form schools while spawning; feed primarily on entomostracans and aquatic insect larvae.

 

Reproduction: One to two spawning events occur during the spring and/or summer months. Fish become sexual mature during first year and are broadcast spawners.

 

Maximum size: 9.8 cm TL (male/unsexed)             

 

Maximum reported age: 3-4 yrs
