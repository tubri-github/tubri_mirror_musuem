Louisiana Fish Species of Concern

Status Report

 

Fundulus euryzonus (Suttkus and Cashner, 1981)

Broadstripe Topminnow

 

                                                                                 Image courtesy of LNHP

                                                                                            

Conservation Status: Louisiana Animal of Conservation Concern; Imperiled because they have a small range in streams in eastern Louisiana and southern Mississippi.

Federal T&E Status: None

State Ranking: S2

NatureServe Global Ranking: G2

Habitat and Biology

Distribution: Endemic to the Lake Pontchartrain drainage and the Tangipahoa and Amite Rivers of Louisiana and Mississippi (Suttkus and Cashner 1981).   

 

Habitat:  A surface oriented species found almost always in close proximity to the shoreline of streams, over an average depth of 46.5 cm and at a current speed of about 7.5cm/s. common around snags, trunks of living trees, and near undercut banks that offer abundant cover in the form of roots (Suttkus and Cashner 1981; Petitfils 1986; Blanchard 1993, 1996; Ross et al. 2001).

Climate/Range: Temperate; 33°N - 31°N (Fishbase)

 

Biology:

            Feeding: Obtains most of its food from the water surface, with adult dipterans (e.g. flies, mosquitoes etc.), ants, spiders, and aphids being most common food items. Prey from the water column is also eaten, especially dipteran larvae and caddisflies (Ross et al. 2001).

           

            Reproduction: Spawning takes place from mid-March through early September, with peak spawning activity in May (Ross et al. 2001).

Morphology:

 

            Body Shape:  A slender topminnow with a pointed snout; caudal fin is rounded; dorsal and ventral outlines of the head are the same, with the head wedge shaped in lateral profile. Adult fish generally range from 40-63mm SL (Blanchard 1996, Ross et al. 2001)

                        Max. size: 69.4mm SL (Suttkus and Cashner 1981)

           

            Life Colors: The back and sides are yellowish to olive with scattered black spots. The underside of the body is unpigmented, except around the anal fin and caudal peduncle. There are concentrations of dark pigment on the underside of the head posterior to the jaw joint. The dorsal fin has numerous distinct spots; spotting on the side varies from sparse to moderately dense. Upper and lower margins of the lateral stripe vary from smooth to rough, with the lower margin usually smooth in females. Pectoral and pelvic fins are generally clear with scattered melanophores (Ross et al. 2001, modified in part from Suttkus and Cashner 1981 ).

           

            External Morphology: The broadstripe topminnow has a broad purplish brown lateral stripe, wide lateral stripe covers at least 50% of the pectoral fin base, width of stripe varies from 33-50% of body depth; a terminal mouth; dorsal fin is set well back on the body. The dorsal fin origin is posterior to the origin of the anal fin; dorsal and anal fins are more elongate in males than females (Ross et al. 2001, modified in part from Suttkus and Cashner 1981).

 

Morphologically similar species: The Broadstripe is most similar to the Blackstripe (Fundulus notatus) and the Blackspotted (Fundulus olivaceus) topminnows. It can be distinguished from both of these by the wide lateral band (usually 40% of the body depth versus 20-33%), by the lack of vertical extensions on the lateral stripe, and by the distinct pigment patches on the underside of the head in back of the lower jaw (Ross et al. 2001).
