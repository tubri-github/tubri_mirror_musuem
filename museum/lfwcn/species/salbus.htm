Louisiana Fish Species of Concern

Status Report

 

 

Scaphirhynchus albus, (Forbes & Richardson, 1905)

Pallid sturgeon

 

picture (Scalb_u2.jpg) by The Native Fish Conservancy

 

Synonymy:

Parascaphirhynchus albus, Forbes & Richardson, 1905

 

Conservation Status:

Considered endangered and rare due to habitat loss, over harvesting and environmental contaminants. Federal agencies expect the population

to become extinct without the introduction of supplemental hatchery stocks.  International trade restricted (CITES II, since Jan 1998)

 

Federal T&E Status: EN

State Ranking: S1

NatureServe Global Ranking: G1

 

 

Habitat and Biology

Distribution: Restricted to the North American Missouri, lower Atchafalaya and Mississippi (Montana to Louisiana) River basins.

 

Habitat: Pallid sturgeon are adapted for living close to the bottom of large, silty rivers with swift currents. The preferred habitat is comprised of sand flats and gravel bars.

 

Biology:

Feeding: Benthic; feed on aquatic insect immatures and small fish.

 

Reproduction: Non-guarders, open sub-stream spawners. Males reach sexual maturity at an estimated 7-9 years, with 2-3 year intervals between spawning events. Females on the other hand reach sexual maturity at an estimated 7-15 years, with up to 10 year intervals between spawning events.

 

Morphology:

Body Shape:  Pallid sturgeon are armored with lengthwise rows of bony plates; The Pallid sturgeon is large and elongate with a flattened, shovel shaped snout, and very small eyes. The caudal peduncel is completely armored and very long. (Ross, 2001)

 

            Maximum size: 200 cm TL (male/unsexed)

 

 

Life Colors:  The coloration is uniformly grayish white.

 

External Morphology: The pallid sturgeon, has fringed and uneven barbels. The bases of the two inner barbels are often in front of the bases of the lateral barbels. The lower lip has fringed lobes, and the upper lip is heavily papillose. Spiracles are absent and belly squamation is usually reduced or absent. (Ross, 2001)

 

 

Morphologically similar species: The pallid sturgeon is most similar to the shovelnose and Alabama sturgeon. Pallid sturgeon can weigh up to 80 pounds, while shovelnose sturgeon reach a maximum weight of 5 pounds and average 2 pounds. The back and sides of the pallid sturgeon are grayish-white versus the brown color of the shovelnose sturgeon. The length of the inner barbels (4 whisker-like appendages in front of the mouth) on a pallid are only about 1/2 as long as the outer barbels, while on the shovelnose all barbels are the same length.
