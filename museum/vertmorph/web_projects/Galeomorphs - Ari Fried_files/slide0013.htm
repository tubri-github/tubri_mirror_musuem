There are a number of sensory features that while not unique in the shark world to Galeomorphs, are nonetheless very important to their success. The two most notable are the Ampullae of Lorenzini, and the lateral line system.  The Ampullae of Lorenzini consist of gel filled pores are found in the head region of the shark, both dorsally, laterally, and ventrally.  These pores contain cells which are stimulated by the presence of external electromagnetic fields created by other living organisms.  Sharks use data from this sensory organ to home in on hidden prey, as well as aiding feeding while the nictitating membrane covers the eye during feeding.  The lateral line system consists of grooves that run laterally along the sharks body just under the skin, mainly at the division of the epaxial and hypaxial muscle and anterior head region.  Special sensory cells within these grooves receive and interpret data on water current and motion, allowing the shark to sense far off struggling prey.
