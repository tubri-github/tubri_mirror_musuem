Modern living sharks evolved from the subclass Neoselachii.  It was during the evolution of this group of organisms that morphological aspects of modern sharks began to emerge.  There were two important adaptations that allowed sharks the impressive agility and power that characterize them today.  One was the increased calcification of the vertebral column, strengthening the trunk of the shark, and allowing for increased power output of the trunk musculature and therefore an increase in speed.  The other was a consolidation of the pectoral girdle, which had a twofold effect.  First it added stability and strength to swimming musculature, second it allowed greater mobility and strength in the jaws, which gained anchor locations for jaw muscles. Also at this time, there began an increase in cranial capacity, as well as a concurrent development of advanced sensory organs.
