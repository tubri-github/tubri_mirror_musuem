What is a Galeomorph?
Galeomorphs are one of two currently living branches of sharks.  As opposed to the more benthically inclined Squalea, Galeomorphs tend toward open water, and more active swimming.  This is typified by perhaps the most famous Galeomorph, Carcharodon carcharias, also known as the Great White.
The view of sharks as supremely adapted hunters comes in no small part to the evolutionary adaptations that characterize Galeomorphs.  While not unique to this branch of sharks, these characteristics are exemplified by them.
