Neoselachii first began the split into Galeomorphs and Squaliformes (the 2 living branches of modern sharks) during the Jurassic time period, which ranged from roughly 210 million years ago to 140 million years ago.  According to the known fossil record however (which while set in stone, it does not set the facts in stone), the greater speciation of Galeomorphs did not occur until the Cretaceous period, from around 140 to 65 million years ago. As Galeomorphs evolved, they further perfected the characteristics that characterized the evolution of Neoselachii.  The vertebral column was further calcified, the pectoral girdle consolidated even more, and a more streamlined body design was adapted, enhancing the classic torpedo body shape.
