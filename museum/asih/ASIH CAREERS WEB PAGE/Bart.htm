Name: Hank Bart

Education: B.S. Biology, University of New Orleans
M.S. Biology, University of New Orleans
Ph.D. Zoology, University of Oklahoma
Website: http://www.tubri.org/museum/museum/reshank.html
Favorite Fish: Ictiobines (A subfamily of suckers)
Favorite Ice Cream: Rocky Road

 

 

How did you first become interested in ichthyology?
Childhood fascination with fish and fishing

What steps did you have to take to get where you are today?
After completing my Ph.D., I worked as a Visiting Assistant Professor at the University of Illinois (teaching postdoc), and an Assistant Professor and Fishes Curator at Auburn University. I got my current job (my dream job) in 1992, 7 years after completing my Ph.D. degree.

What kinds of skills are required for your career? What experiences do you feel prepared you best for your job?
Museum curation, knowledge of vertebrates in general and fish in particular, experience with morphology-based systematics, field experience.

What are your responsibilities?
I curate one of the largest fish collections in the world. I supervise a staff of 5 people who assist with this task. I teach upper-level courses on the Tulane campus (including general ichthyology) and train graduate students.

What do you like best about your career?
I really love collecting fish, and studying fish specimens in the lab. I am also dedicated to conservation of imperiled fishes.

What would be your most important piece of advice for someone considering the career you're in now?
I know this sounds cliché, but the key to success is determination and hard work. In the academic arena, it is also important to be collegial and a good communicator. Of course, one also has to do good work.
