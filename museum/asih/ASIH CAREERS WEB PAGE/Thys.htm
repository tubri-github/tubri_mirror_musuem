Name: Tierney Thys
Current Position: Science Editor, Sea Studios Foundation
Education: B.A. Biology, Brown University, RI
Ph.D. Zoology, Duke University, NC
Website: www.shapeoflife.com
Favorite Fish: Molidae, Syngnathidae, Mobulinae. . . in truth just about every single fish out there.
Favorite Ice Cream: Sadly enough I can't really stomach ice cream but prefer grilled cheese and tomato.

 


How did you first become interested in ichthyology?
Aside from having gills in utero, it just seemed a natural. The fishes make and break every rule-an admirable trait. They are as wildly creative as you can be whilst remaining a vertebrate.

What steps did you have to take to get where you are today?
College and grad school were tremendously important-especially the advice and encouragement I was given by my family, advisors and committee members. The opportunities my grad school advisor offered me led to my present job.

What kinds of skills are required for your career? What experiences do you feel prepared you best for your job?
I work in an extremely collaborative environment-it literally takes a small army to make even a small film. So being comfortable working on a team is very important. The ability to assimilate an enormous amount of material, weave a fast story and persuasively paint a picture as to why the subject matter is worth covering-those are important skills as well. I draw on editing skills constantly.

What are your responsibilities?
I wear several hats here since we are a tiny company. Some of my responsibilities include:
Story developer for new projects
Locating the perfect character for the story
Writing NSF grants and many others in search of funding
Checking and rechecking that we've got our facts straight.
Designing educational outreach materials to accompany our broadcast media.

What do you like best about your career?
My job has tremendous variety, the material I deal with is constantly intellectually challenging and thought-provoking and my co-workers are tremendous

What would be your most important piece of advice for someone considering the career you're in now?
Be patient yet tenacious.
Attend film festivals showing the films you most want to make.
Be bold and make contacts.
Try to get an AAAS mass media fellowship in television.
Talk to as many people as you can in the field. I have a very rosy view of working in television-many people might have quite a different view from me.

Good luck!
