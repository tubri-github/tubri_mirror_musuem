U.S. Geological Survey
Monitor, analyze, interpret, and disseminate information on
earthquakes, volcanoes, and the geology and topography of
the United States.
Monitor and assess water quality, streamflows and ground water
at thousands of sites across the nation
Produce more than 100,000 different maps
Estimate world and United States energy and mineral supplies

Conduct a wide range of research on biology, geology, and water
to provide land and resource managers information they need to
make sound decisions, and to help mitigate the effects of
natural hazards.
