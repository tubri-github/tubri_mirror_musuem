This course examines the theory and practice of natural resource
preservation in the United States, and the wide spectrum of agencies
and organizations involved in this endeavor.
Focus will be on activities occurring within the State of Louisiana,
especially the New Orleans vicinity.
We will begin with a discussion of basic principles of ecology and
conservation biology.
Will then review geology, climate, and ecoregions of Louisiana.
We will examine land ownership in Louisiana and extents to which
different habitats/ecoregions are protected.
Course Overview and Objectives continued
This will be followed by review of the agencies involved in natural
resource conservation and the environmental laws that guide activities
of these agencies.
