Approximately 25% of the state's surface is raised
Pleistocene terraces, consisting mostly of sand and gravel.
Pleistocene surfaces are remnants of preexisting flood plains.
They were raised as the coastal plain titled in response to
downwraping of the crustal floor of the Gulf of Mexico.
Geology continued
Older (Tertiary) deposits confined to northern portion of the
state; decrease in age coastward.
