Status of Land Development
in Louisiana
Most of the land area of Louisiana converted to
agriculture long age; increasing conversion of private
land to forest products culture in more recent times.
Almost all of the wet and mesic coastal prairies have been lost; coastal marsh disappearing at an alarming rate.
Virtually every stream in the Mississippi Alluvial Plain has
been channelized, levied or otherwise hydraulically altered.
Bottomland hardwood forest extensive, but privately owned, declining.
