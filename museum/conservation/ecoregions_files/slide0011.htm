Public Land in the U.S.
Public lands cover more than 600 million acres in the U.S.
—almost one third of the country!  Include national parks,
national seashores, national wildlife refuges, wilderness areas,
national forests, monuments, select lakes and seashores,
marine sanctuaries, historic and scenic trails and national
grasslands.
Most public land is concentrated in the western U.S. Nearly 83%
of Nevada; 68%of Alaska; 64%of Utah; 61% of Idaho; 52%of
Oregon; 49% of Wyoming; and 44% of California is public land
managed by the federal government.
