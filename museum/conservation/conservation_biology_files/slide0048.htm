Molecular genetics
Need to characterize populations genetically and assess
genetic variability led to development of new molecular
techniques.
Protein electrophoresis allowed researchers to infer
differences in presumptive gene loci by examining mobility
of specific proteins (allozymes) on starch gels.
Methods developed in the 1980’s for extracting DNA from
cells, cutting it at specific places and eventually sequencing
it for direct measures of genic variation.
