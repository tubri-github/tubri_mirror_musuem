Habitat Conservation Planning
In order to gain an incidental-take permit, landowners have
to prepare a habitat conservation plan (HCP) that
explains how development action would affect the
endangered species, and how the effects would be
mitigated.  HCP’s became very popular in 1990’s.
“No-surprises” clause added in 1994; once HCP approved
landowner not responsible for unforeseen circumstances.
No-surprises policy extended to all owners of land with
endangered species as a way of encouraging development
of HCP’s.
