Criticism of HCP’s and no surprises
Scientists complained that ecological systems are
characteristically uncertain; “no-surprises” meant agencies
couldn’t change HCP to adapt to changing conditions.
“No surprises” policy did not require any conservation
commitment on part of land owner.
Landowners now enter into Memoranda of Agreements
(MOA) and Safe-harbor agreements, in which they agree
to provide and/or manage habitat for predetermined
number of individuals of an endangered species in
exchange for incidental take permit.
