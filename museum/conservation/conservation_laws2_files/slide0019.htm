Scientific criticism of ESA
ESA focused on individual species and their habitat; very
expensive, applied too late (crisis management), and
almost always contentious (reactive rather than proactive).
In 2000, FWS spent entire budget for listing and recovery
on legal fees; Bush administration has limited lawsuits.
ESA emphasizes “small-population paradigm”; doesn’t
protect patchily distributed species (metapopulations);
lacks thresholds for establishing when species has
recovered.
Value of species and habitat determined through cost-benefit
analysis; pits natural heritage against market values.
