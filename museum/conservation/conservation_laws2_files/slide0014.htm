Problems with ESA cont’d
In many cases the habitat of endangered species is entirely
on privately owned land.
Landowners became fearful that presence of endangered
species would lead to restrictions on how they could use
their land; began to manage land in manner that wouldn’t
support the species (e.g, red cockaded woodpecker). 
In an effort to avoid such “environmental train wrecks”,
ESA amended in 1982 to allow permitting of “incidental
takes”.
