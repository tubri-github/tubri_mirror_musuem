Population growth
Population growth rate (r) = intrinsic rate of
increase (Malthusian parameter)
In closed population, determined by
birth and death rates:
r = b - d
 where :
b = births (natality)
d = deaths (mortality)

Slide 5

Exponential Growth
dN/dt = rN
Change in population size (N) over
time period (t) equals growth rate
times population size
Unchecked population with unlimited
resources grows exponentially

Slide 7

Logistic growth
Eventually, resources become limiting and/or
negative interactions among members of the
population start to occur.
So equation has to be modified to describe
carrying capacity (K) of environment at which
point no net growth in population occurs
dN/dt = rN(K-N)
  K
as N gets close to K, growth slows and dN/dt ® 0

Factors contributing to
carrying capacity
Limits can be related to number of individuals in the
population - density dependent factors
e.g. resource competition
Or some factor external to population - density
independent
e.g. frost, flood, fire, locust plague

Life history strategies
Recognition of different kinds of factors limiting populations (extrinsic vs. intrinsic) led to belief that populations could evolve different life history strategies.
