RARE AND PROTECTED FISHES PROJECT
LNHP-Tulane University-ULM
      The LNHP considers 27 fish species as species of concern due to their rarity or vulnerability. Two are presently listed as federally Threatened or Endangered (Gulf sturgeon, pallid sturgeon).  Knowing their status would allow the state to take conservation actions.
Goal
Update existing records in the LNHP database and survey additional sites in an attempt to add new records to the database.
Approach
Systematically visit streams known to have these rare or vulnerable fish species in the state.

LANDOWNERS

Implementation of Red-Cockaded Woodpecker (RCW) Safe Harbor Program
The Red-cockaded Woodpecker is indigenous to longleaf pine forest of southeastern United States.
Small, isolated, and genetically important groups do exist on private lands in Louisiana but their probability of existence is very low if these fragmented forest are not managed for the RCW.
RCW located on private lands have presented problems for landowners.
Certain management actions  are unlawful under the Endangered Species Act.

"Goal"
   Goal
Implement the RCW Safe Harbor Program on non-federal lands in Louisiana and in doing so help landowners by easing some of the restrictions in place on RCW habitat.
   Approach
    A staff biologist will start implementing this program in the next months. At that time, he will start meeting with interested landowners to explain the benefits of enrolling in the Safe Harbor Program.
