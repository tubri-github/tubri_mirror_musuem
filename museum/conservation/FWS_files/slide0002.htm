U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service
Operates 66 National Fish Hatcheries, 64 fishery resource
offices and 78 ecological services field stations; restores
nationally significant fisheries.
Also:
• Enforces federal wildlife laws
• Protects endangered species
• Manages migratory birds
• Conserves and restores wildlife habitat
• Helps foreign governments with international conservation
  efforts.
• Distributes hundreds of millions of dollars in excise taxes
  on fishing and hunting equipment to State fish and wildlife
  agencies.
