Interstate Protection, Refuges
1900. The Lacey Act becomes the first Federal law
protecting game, prohibiting the interstate shipment of
illegally taken wildlife and importation of species.
1903. The first Federal Bird Reservation is established by
President Theodore Roosevelt on Pelican Island, Florida.
Pelican Island and other early Federal wildlife reservations
are re- designated as "national wildlife refuges" in 1942.
1918. Migratory Bird Treaty Act is passed implementing
convention Between the United States and Canada for
protecting migratory birds, regulating of migratory bird
hunting.
