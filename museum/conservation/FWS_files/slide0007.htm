U.S. Fish and Fisheries  Commission
1871. U.S. Commission on Fish and Fisheries is created
by Congress and charged with studying and recommending
solutions to the decline in food fishes and to promote fish
culture.
Spencer Fullerton Baird is appointed as the first
Commissioner.
A year later, the Commission's Baird Station in
northern California is used to collect, fertilize and
ship salmon eggs by rail to the East Coast.
