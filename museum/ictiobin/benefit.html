Expected benefit of this project

Subfamily Ictiobinae is an important group of large river fishes.  It is the only subfamily of suckers that has yet to undergo comprehensive systematic review, yet it is key to understanding early catostomid evolution. Fossil evidence (Smith 1981) and the high degrees of sympatry of currently recognized ictiobine species suggest that most of the species diverged long ago, yet there is clear evidence of interspecific hybridization, especially within the Mississippi River Basin. All of this contributes to the taxonomic confusion that presently exists within the group.  This project will use both traditional (species descriptions and taxonomic revision) and modern methods of systematics (morphometrics and molecular sequencing) to increase understanding of diversity and interrelationships within in the subfamily. It will help to eliminate some of the confusion that persists from previous treatments, and will provide characters useful for the identification of species both as adults and early life stages.  It will provide information on genetic variability within species, the role of interspecifc hybridization in genetic structuring, and possibly the extent, direction and causes of genetic introgression. It will establish phylogenies for genera and species complexes based on independent molecular sequence data, and will address patterns of evolution in the mitochondrial and nuclear genomes. The phylogenies will be useful for interpreting trends in morphological evolution.  Moreover, we will be able to use the derived phylogenies to address questions about patterns and rates of speciation and dispersal in large rivers.

Students at two stages of professional development (undergraduate and graduate) will receive training in both traditional and modern methods of systematics as a result of this project.  This web site will be used to inform ichthyologists about the results of this revision as they become available.  Ultimately, taxonomic keys, images of types, illustrations, photographs, record of museum holdings, a bibliography, and other results of the project will be posted at this site.
